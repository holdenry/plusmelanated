BLACK GODDESS OF THE MONTH

Regina Everett-Johnson

Of the Central New Jersey Area

Mrs. Regina Johnson is a native of New Jersey who attended Bloomfield College where she received her BA degree in Criminal Justice and Sociology. Since graduating, she has worked as a social worker for a number of community nonprofits. Regina is currently employed as a Director of Activities in a nursing home facility in the Central New Jersey area. She has worked there for 16 years. Mrs. Johnson has been married for 30 years; she and her husband have one daughter. Regina has been a loyal and active member of the Macedonia Baptist Church where she acts as a Deaconess.

Through this religious institution, she has volunteered for their Soup Kitchen to provide meals for the needy and prepare and distribute Thanksgiving dinners for approximately 1,700 community residents each year. Additionally, Regina volunteers with the NJ Orators of Ocean County, Jack and Jill of America, the NAACP of Ocean County and as a Chaplin of the Ocean County Cotillion. At other times, Regina can be found reading her bible; staying informed about local and national politics; mentoring the youth; traveling with her family and expanding her knowledge about neurology.
